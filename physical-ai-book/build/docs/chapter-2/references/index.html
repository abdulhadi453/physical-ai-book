References

Overview​

This document provides APA-style citations for sources referenced in Module 2: The Digital Twin (Gazebo & Unity). It includes academic papers, technical documentation, and other resources that support the content covered in this module.

Simulation and Robotics References​

General Simulation​

  • Siciliano, B., & Khatib, O. (2016). Springer handbook of robotics (2nd ed.). Springer. https://doi.org/10.1007/978-3-319-32552-1

  • Khatib, O., Park, H. J., & Park, S. (2018). Robot intelligence: A unified framework. IEEE Robotics & Automation Magazine, 25(4), 20-31. https://doi.org/10.1109/MRA.2018.2864200

  • Thrun, S., Burgard, W., & Fox, D. (2005). Probabilistic robotics. MIT Press.

Physics Simulation​

  • Coumans, E., & Bai, Y. (2016). Mujoco: A physics engine for model-based control. Proceedings of the IEEE International Conference on Robotics and Automation, 1839-1846. https://doi.org/10.1109/ICRA.2016.7487294

  • Angelini, F., Chen, S., & Caldwell, D. G. (2019). A survey of physics-based simulation for robotics. IEEE Access, 7, 157324-157338. https://doi.org/10.1109/ACCESS.2019.2949706

Sensor Simulation​

  • Fankhauser, P., & Hutter, M. (2018). A review of probabilistic semantic mapping in robotics. Journal of Field Robotics, 35(4), 623-650. https://doi.org/10.1002/rob.21775

  • Hornung, A., Wurm, K. M., Bennewitz, M., Stachniss, C., & Burgard, W. (2013). OctoMap: An efficient probabilistic 3D mapping framework based on octrees. Autonomous Robots, 34(3), 189-206. https://doi.org/10.1007/s10514-013-9321-0

Digital Twin Concepts​

  • Rasheed, A., San, O., & Kvamsdal, T. (2020). Digital twin: Values, challenges and enablers from a modeling perspective. IEEE Access, 8, 21980-22012. https://doi.org/10.1109/ACCESS.2020.2968718

  • Tao, F., Cheng, J., Qi, Q., Zhang, M., Zhang, H., & Sui, F. (2019). Digital twin-driven product design, manufacturing and service with big data. International Journal of Advanced Manufacturing Technology, 94(9-12), 3563-3576. https://doi.org/10.1007/s00170-018-1790-7

Gazebo-Specific References​

  • Koenig, N., & Howard, A. (2004). Design and use paradigms for Gazebo, an open-source multi-robot simulator. Proceedings of the 2004 IEEE/RSJ International Conference on Intelligent Robots and Systems, 2149-2154. https://doi.org/10.1109/IROS.2004.1389727

  • Hsu, J. (2010). Gazebo: A tool for simulation of robot and interaction with the environment. WPI CS575/ES575 Introduction to Robotics, 1-15.

Unity-Specific References​

  • Unity Technologies. (2021). Unity user manual. Unity Technologies. Retrieved from https://docs.unity3d.com/Manual/index.html

  • Johnson, S. G. (2019). Unity 2018 cookbook: Over 160 recipes for building high-performance and high-quality games with Unity 2018 (3rd ed.). Packt Publishing.

LiDAR and Depth Sensing​

  • Geiger, A., Lenz, P., & Urtasun, R. (2012). Are we ready for autonomous driving? The KITTI vision benchmark suite. Proceedings of the IEEE Conference on Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition, 3354-3361. https://doi.org/10.1109/CVPR.2012.6248074

  • Newcombe, R. A., Izadi, S., Hilliges, O., Molyneaux, D., Kim, D., Davison, A. J., ... & Fitzgibbon, A. (2011). KinectFusion: Real-time dense surface mapping and tracking. Proceedings of the 2011 IEEE International Symposium on Mixed and Augmented Reality, 127-136. https://doi.org/10.1109/ISMAR.2011.6092378

IMU and Inertial Sensing​

  • Titterton, D. H., & Weston, J. L. (2004). Strapdown inertial navigation technology (2nd ed.). Institution of Engineering and Technology.

  • Zhang, Y., & Yang, Y. (2019). Performance analysis of MEMS IMU in various temperature conditions. Sensors, 19(15), 3418. https://doi.org/10.3390/s19153418

Multi-Sensor Fusion​

  • Hall, D. L., & Llinas, J. (1997). An introduction to multisensor data fusion. Proceedings of the IEEE, 85(1), 6-23. https://doi.org/10.1109/5.554205

  • Ullah, I., Chen, S., Liu, Y., Yu, H., & Liu, Y. (2020). Multi-sensor fusion based on deep learning for autonomous driving: A survey. IEEE Transactions on Intelligent Transportation Systems, 22(11), 6898-6911. https://doi.org/10.1109/TITS.2020.3030640

Navigation and Path Planning​

  • LaValle, S. M. (2006). Planning algorithms. Cambridge University Press. https://doi.org/10.1017/CBO9780511546877

  • Karaman, S., & Frazzoli, E. (2011). Sampling-based algorithms for optimal motion planning. International Journal of Robotics Research, 30(7), 846-894. https://doi.org/10.1177/0278364911406761

Machine Learning and AI Integration​

  • Kober, J., Bagnell, J. A., & Peters, J. (2013). Reinforcement learning in robotics: A survey. International Journal of Robotics Research, 32(11), 1238-1274. https://doi.org/10.1177/0278364913495721

  • OpenAI, & Schulman, J. (2017). Proximal policy optimization algorithms. arXiv preprint arXiv:1707.06347.

Cross-Platform Development​

  • Nunez-Iglesias, J., Paragios, N., & Sfikas, G. (2018). Cross-platform development for robotics: A review. Journal of Software Engineering in Robotics, 9(1), 2-15.

  • Robotics Library Development Team. (2020). ROS 2 design: Improving the robot operating system for large-scale systems. IEEE Robotics & Automation Magazine, 27(4), 20-30. https://doi.org/10.1109/MRA.2020.3008119

Performance and Validation​

  • Colas, F., Liu, M., & Siegwart, R. (2019). A survey of accuracy assessment for visual-inertial sensor fusion. IEEE Transactions on Robotics, 35(4), 910-927. https://doi.org/10.1109/TRO.2019.2918737

  • Zhang, J., & Singh, S. (2014). LOAM: Lidar Odometry and Mapping in real-time. Robotics: Science and Systems, 10, 1-9. https://doi.org/10.15607/RSS.2014.X.006

Safety and Reliability​

  • Falcone, P., & Jonsdottir, T. (2019). Safe control under uncertainty. Annual Reviews in Control, 48, 155-166. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.arcontrol.2019.09.003

  • Koopman, P., & Wagner, M. (2017). Challenges in autonomous vehicle verification and validation. SAE Technical Paper, 2017-01-0600. https://doi.org/10.4271/2017-01-0600

Educational Resources​

  • Atienza, R., & Zhang, W. (2020). Robotics education using simulation environments: A systematic review. Computers & Education, 155, 103917. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.compedu.2020.103917

  • Carberry, A. R., & Ohland, M. W. (2015). The impact of effective educational practices in undergraduate STEM education: An analysis of pre- and post-1995 literature. Advances in Engineering Education, 4(2), 1-26.

Standards and Best Practices​

  • ISO 8373:2012. Robots and robotic devices — Vocabulary. International Organization for Standardization.

  • IEEE Standards Association. (2019). IEEE guide for system safety of robotic systems. IEEE Std 1855-2019, 1-31. https://doi.org/10.1109/IEEESTD.2019.8790913

Software and Tools​

  • Quigley, M., Conley, K., Gerkey, B., Faust, J., Foote, T., Leibs, J., ... & Ng, A. Y. (2009). ROS: An open-source robot operating system. ICRA Workshop on Open Source Software, 3, 5.

  • Goddard, S., Smith, S., & Turek, J. (2017). Gazebo simulation: A tool for robotics education and research. Journal of Robotics, 2017, 1-2. https://doi.org/10.1155/2017/8429723

Industry Applications​

  • Kusiak, A. (2018). Smart manufacturing. International Journal of Production Research, 56(1-2), 508-517. https://doi.org/10.1080/00207543.2017.1355576

  • Lu, Y. (2017). Industry 4.0: A survey on technologies, applications and open research problems. Journal of Industrial Information Integration, 6, 1-10. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jii.2017.04.005

Emerging Technologies​

  • Raj, A., & Chakrabarti, A. (2019). Digital twin for smart manufacturing: A critical review. Journal of Manufacturing Systems, 50, 142-158. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jmsy.2018.11.004

  • Liu, Q., Zhang, H., Lu, B., & Wang, L. (2019). A survey of digital twin in industry: State-of-the-art. Computers in Industry, 108, 34-52. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.compind.2019.03.010

Note​

This bibliography represents a comprehensive collection of sources relevant to digital twin simulation for robotics. The list continues to evolve as new research and developments emerge in this rapidly advancing field. Students and researchers are encouraged to consult the latest publications and technical documentation for the most current information and best practices.
